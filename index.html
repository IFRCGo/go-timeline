Following the announcement of the full withdrawal of US troops from Afghanistan by 11 September 2021, territorial control is rapidly shifting. The number of displaced people begins to increase, and many seek refuge in larger cities across the country.

18 August 2021

IFRC allocated 168,483 Swiss francs from the Disaster Relief Emergency Fund (DREF) to enable Iranian Red Crescent to initiate a preparedness and response operation for cross-border population movement.

26 August 2021

IFRC allocated 348,761 Swiss francs from the DREF to support the Red Crescent Society of Tajikistan in their preparedness efforts to respond to a potential cross-border population movement from Afghanistan into Tajikistan.

6 September 2021

IFRC issues Revised Emergency Appeal No. 2 for the operation in Afghanistan, increasing the funding requirement from 15 million Swiss francs to 36 million Swiss francs: to enable the ARCS to deliver assistance and support to 560,000 people over 24 months. A second DREF allocation of additional 500,000 Swiss francs has been approved which brings the total DREF allocation for this operation to 1 million Swiss francs.

7 September 2021

Formation of an interim government in Afghanistan.

13 September 2021

IFRC allocated 94,505 Swiss francs from the DREF to support the Pakistan Red Crescent to initiate preparedness activities for cross-border population movement including first aid trainings and a cash feasibility study.

25 September 2021

IFRC issues Multi-country Emergency Appeal for 24.6 million Swiss francs for 160,000 people.
